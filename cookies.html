Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 8, 2026

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Simply Complex Solutions ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as:

  • User authentication and session management
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Load balancing and server performance
  • Remembering your preferences and settings

These cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the service to function.

Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Error messages encountered
  • Load times and performance metrics
  • Traffic sources and navigation patterns

Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow us to remember choices you make and provide enhanced features:

  • Language and regional preferences
  • Customized dashboard layouts
  • Autofill information for forms
  • Recent searches and viewed items

Analytics Cookies

We use analytics tools to understand how users interact with our services:

  • Track website traffic and user behavior
  • Measure marketing campaign effectiveness
  • Identify popular features and content
  • Generate aggregated usage statistics

5. Similar Technologies

Local Storage

We use browser local storage to store larger amounts of data needed for the application to function properly, including cached data and user preferences.

Pixel Tags

Small transparent images that help us understand how users interact with our website and marketing emails.

Device Fingerprinting

We may collect device information for security purposes to detect and prevent fraudulent activity.

6. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages:

  • Google Analytics: Website usage analytics
  • Google Ads: Conversion tracking and remarketing
  • Facebook: Advertising and conversion tracking
  • Intercom: Customer support chat
  • Gumroad: Payment processing

7. Managing Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from all or specific websites
  • Set preferences for first-party and third-party cookies

Instructions for popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings ‚Üí Privacy and Security ‚Üí Cookies
  • Firefox: Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Cookies
  • Edge: Settings ‚Üí Cookies and Site Permissions

Opt-Out Tools

  • Google Analytics: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
  • Network Advertising Initiative: http://www.networkadvertising.org/choices

8. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable or delete cookies:

  • Some features may not work properly
  • You may need to log in more frequently
  • Your preferences may not be saved
  • The website may load more slowly

9. Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for handling DNT signals, so we do not respond to DNT signals at this time.

10. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, we will display a cookie banner requesting your consent to use non-essential cookies. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences

11. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our business practices. We will notify you of any material changes.
